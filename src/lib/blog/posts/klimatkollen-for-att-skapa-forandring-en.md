Klimatkollen's data is open and accessible to all, so that citizens can hold those in power and decision-makers accountable. Our data gives you the facts you need when engaging with politicians, local council officials, and companies on climate issues.

## Download our guide

Since we launched in 2022, we've received many examples of how our data has been used by citizens and civil society, and here we share some of them — which may inspire you to act for change!

The tips are also available in a [guide](/documents/Klimatkollen_user_guide_EN.pdf) that you can download and share with others, or print out and tick off.

## 10 ways to use Klimatkollen to create change

### Influence companies' climate work

1. **Contact companies**

   On our [company pages](https://klimatkollen.se/en/explore/companies), find out which companies have increased their emissions, or do not report emissions at all. Contact them and ask how they plan to reduce their emissions, or when they will start reporting them. Do they have offices where you live? Organise a demonstration outside. If you are a shareholder, attend annual general meetings and ask critical questions about their climate work.

2. **Drive change at work**

   Drive sustainability efforts where you work. In sustainability reports on Klimatkollen, you can read about companies' climate initiatives in your industry. Get inspired and bring your own ideas to your workplace. You can also download our data via the [Klimatkollen API](https://klimatkollen.se/en/products).

   Example: [Gidås Hållbarhetsbyrå](https://www.instagram.com/p/DSxA_dmjO6D/).

### Hold your local politicians to account

3. **Question politicians**

   Find out how emissions and the climate transition are progressing in your [municipality](https://klimatkollen.se/en/municipalities) and [region](https://klimatkollen.se/en/regions) on Klimatkollen, then put questions to politicians and civil servants. Do they monitor their emissions? Do they have measurable emissions targets, and how are these followed up? Is there a climate plan — and if not, when will there be one?

   Example: [Ett hållbart Lidingö](https://www.instagram.com/p/DWVZTwZDKV9/).

4. **Participate in debates**

   Are you going to listen to a debate or public hearing, or perhaps participate yourself? Formulate questions and talking points based on our data and let facts guide the discussion.

5. **Demonstrate**

   Display figures and rankings from our lists on signs, banners and leaflets at municipal council meetings and peaceful demonstrations.

   Example: [Naturskyddsföreningen i Tranås](https://www.instagram.com/p/DCyQS_RsrIg/?img_index=1).

6. **Write a citizens' proposal**

   Draw on the climate data for your municipality, and look at other municipalities' climate plans for ideas on climate action.

   Example: [Ready-made proposals and templates from Klimatklubben](https://klimatklubben.se/medborgarforslag/) (in Swedish).

7. **Join a campaign**

   Participate in and spread campaigns that use our statistics as a starting point — or start your own.

   Example: [The People's Voice campaign](https://klimatklubben.se/engagemang/folkets-rost/).

### Carry out your own analyses and share the data

8. **Create your own initiatives**

   Use the data as a starting point and do your own analyses, combine it with other data and cross-analyse.

   Example: [“Climate slimming tool”](https://www.instagram.com/p/DLErHhDMWDy/).

9. **Reach out through media**

   Use the data as inspiration and source material for articles and opinion pieces in local media and member publications, or for radio programmes and podcasts. Write yourself or tip off journalists — and write about something that relates to your organisation, municipality, county, or the companies in your area.

   Example: [P4 Jönköping on biking data](https://www.instagram.com/p/DRwgZRcjBgK/).

10. **Share on social media**

    Follow us and like, comment, save, and share our posts so that more people gain climate knowledge. Create your own posts — for example, take screenshots from the site or use our top lists to find angles that are relevant locally to where you live.

    You'll find our social media channels at the bottom of the site.

Do you have your own examples of how you've used our climate data? We'd love to hear from you — email us at hej@klimatkollen.se.
